# ASA 7 Use Cases

## Purpose

This document gives public-safe examples of where ASA 7 style observer-layer observability becomes useful.

## Use Case 1 - Long-Horizon Agent Stability

Long-horizon systems may remain locally coherent while accumulating deeper instability across many steps.

ASA 7 is relevant when the question becomes:

- does the same instability pattern recur before obvious collapse?

## Use Case 2 - Structural Failure Under Pressure

Some advanced systems may fail not because of one content topic, but because of repeated structural pressure.

ASA 7 is relevant when the question becomes:

- does a similar pressure profile repeatedly produce the same fracture pattern?

## Use Case 3 - Boundary Behavior Research

Some systems appear stable until they repeatedly approach the same interpretive edge.

ASA 7 is relevant when the question becomes:

- do threshold transitions reappear across reruns, paraphrases, and shifted surface language?

## Use Case 4 - Recovery and Reversibility

An important research question is whether systems lose the ability to reopen earlier interpretive flexibility after repeated perturbation.

ASA 7 is relevant when the question becomes:

- does recovery compress after repeated return to the same edge condition?

## Use Case 5 - Structural vs Topic Sensitivity

Not every failure is topic-driven.

ASA 7 is relevant when the question becomes:

- does the instability depend more on structure than on subject matter?

## Public Summary

ASA 7 is most useful when one needs to study:

- repeatable instability
- threshold behavior
- recovery compression
- structural recurrence
- observer-layer boundary behavior across controlled variation
