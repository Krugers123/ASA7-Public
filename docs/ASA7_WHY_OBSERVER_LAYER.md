# ASA 7 Why Observer-Layer

## Purpose

This document explains why ASA 7 studies observer-layer behavior rather than relying only on output-level evaluation.

## Why Output-Only Reading Is Too Weak

Many important AI failure modes do not first appear as obvious error.

They may first appear as:

- narrowing under pressure
- loss of recovery flexibility
- repeated fracture shapes
- boundary instability
- directional drift that remains locally coherent

An output-only approach often misses that deeper structure.

## Why Observer-Layer Matters

Observer-layer reading focuses on:

- how interpretation behaves across time
- how pressure accumulates
- whether the same boundary conditions recur
- whether structural patterns survive wording changes

This makes it possible to study:

- trajectory condition
- repeatability of fracture shapes
- structural sensitivity vs topic sensitivity

## Public Value

The observer-layer frame gives ASA 7 a stronger research position than ordinary anomaly tracking.

It shifts the question from:

- what weird output appeared once?

to:

- what structured instability recurs under controlled pressure?

That is the public-safe research edge of ASA 7.
